<p><p>The concrete slab market can be divided into two primary types: hollow slabs and solid slabs. Hollow slabs are lightweight and have voids that reduce material usage and improve thermal insulation, making them cost-effective for large spans in construction. Solid slabs, on the other hand, are denser and provide greater strength and durability, making them suitable for heavy loads and high-traffic areas. Both types serve different structural needs in residential, commercial, and industrial applications.</p></p>
